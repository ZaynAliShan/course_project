The Basics of Scrum

Scrum is simple light weighted agile project management methodology that enables product teams to build products in an iterative fashion through effective team collaboration. This is done by Sprints. A sprint is a short, time-boxed period when a scrum team works to complete a set amount of work i.e. a project.

What are the Scrum Roles?

The scrum team is made up of three roles: A Product Owner, a Scrum Master and the Development Team:

  • Product Owner

    • A Product Owner in a scrum team decides what needs to be built. This person has complete knowledge about the market and business needs, has a vision.

  • Scrum Master

    • The Scrum Master is not a management title and cannot make decisions on behalf of the team. The Scrum Master's major responsibility is to ensure that the team stays on track. He should understand the different skill sets of his team and assign them to tasks which they are best at to ensure the product is delivered well and before the dealine. A Scrum Master should guide the team such that the team does not go astray.

  • Development Team

    • The Development team in scrum is the team that has all the skills necessary to execute the backlog items. With the help of their scrum masters, the team members shares their special skills and knowledge to try and fulfil the objective.
